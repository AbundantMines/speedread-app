Find your information bottleneck

The average executive reads at 230–280 WPM. The outliers — the ones who always seem to have the right data point — are often reading at 400–600 WPM. Find out exactly where you stand.

Most people read at the same speed they learned to read as a child. Between third and fifth grade, the brain settles into a pace — comfortable, reliable, and frozen there for the next several decades. Nobody teaches you to go faster. Nobody tells you the ceiling is far higher than you think.

The average adult reads around 238 words per minute. That sounds reasonable until you do the math. A typical nonfiction book holds about 70,000 words. At your current pace, that is nearly five hours of uninterrupted reading. Most people never finish the books they start — not for lack of interest, but because the time cost feels impossible.

RSVP stands for Rapid Serial Visual Presentation. Instead of your eyes scanning across a line, words appear one at a time, precisely centered in your field of vision. Your gaze stays fixed. There is no backtracking, no losing your place, no drifting between lines. Freed from the mechanics of eye movement, your brain directs all of its energy toward meaning.

The surprising thing is how quickly the rhythm becomes natural. Most first-time users feel a flicker of discomfort. By the second session, something begins to click. Within two weeks of ten-minute daily practice, the average user reads thirty to forty percent faster with no measurable drop in comprehension.

The skill is learnable. The ceiling is higher than you think. And every minute you recover belongs entirely to you.
