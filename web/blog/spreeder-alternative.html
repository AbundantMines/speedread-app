Spreeder has been around since the early days of RSVP speed reading. If you've tried speed reading before, you've probably used it. But "first" doesn't mean "best," and the speed reading app landscape has changed significantly.

If you're looking for a Spreeder alternative — whether because of pricing, limited features, or a dated interface — here's an honest comparison with WarpReader.

What Spreeder Gets Right (and Wrong)

What works

  • Established brand — Spreeder has been around long enough that most speed reading articles mention it
  • Structured courses — A gamified training program with levels and achievements
  • iOS app — Native mobile experience
  • Basic RSVP — The core word-flashing mechanism works

What doesn't

  • No PDF or EPUB support — You can't upload documents. This is a dealbreaker for anyone who reads PDFs, research papers, or ebooks.
  • No Chrome extension — You can't speed read webpages in place. Every article requires manual copy-paste.
  • Dated interface — The design hasn't evolved meaningfully. It looks and feels like a 2015 web app.
  • Expensive — $9.99/month or $79.99/year for features that other apps offer at half the price or free.
  • Heavy free-tier restrictions — The free version is so limited it's essentially a demo, not a useful tool.
  • No speed test — You have to find your baseline elsewhere before you can train effectively.
